Privacy Policy for Buttons 2: Gorilla & Pig

Last updated: August 14, 2025

Buttons 2: Gorilla & Pig is a mobile game developed by mustakuusi. Your privacy is important to us. This Privacy Policy explains how information is collected, used, and disclosed when you use our app.

Information Collection and Use

We do not collect any personally identifiable information directly from users. However, our app uses third-party services that may automatically collect certain information to provide advertisements, analytics, and game features, including:

  • Device information (e.g., model, OS version)
  • Usage data (e.g., session duration, feature usage)
  • Advertising ID
  • Player ID and gameplay activity via Google Play Games Services

Advertising and Children

Our app uses Unity Ads to show ads. When the app is used by children under the age of 13 (or the relevant age in your country), Unity Ads will only display non-behavioral ads, in compliance with Google Play’s Families Policy. Advertising ID is used for ad measurement, frequency capping, and non-personalized ad serving. It is not used to personally identify users.

Third-Party Services

Our app uses the following third-party services, which may collect and use data according to their own privacy policies:

  • Unity Ads (for advertisements) – Unity Privacy Policy
  • Google Play Games Services (for achievements, leaderboards, and cloud saves) – Google Play Privacy Policy

How We Use Data

Data collected by these services may be used to:

  • Serve relevant or non-personalized advertisements
  • Provide game features such as achievements and leaderboards
  • Improve app performance and user experience

Opt-Out and Settings

Users can manage or opt out of certain data collection:

  • Unity Ads: Follow instructions on the Unity Privacy Policy page to manage ad preferences or opt out of personalized ads
  • Google Play Games: Sign out or manage permissions in your Google account settings

Children’s Privacy

We do not knowingly collect personally identifiable information from children under 13 (or the applicable age in your country). If you believe we have collected personal data from a child, please contact us, and we will take steps to remove it.
